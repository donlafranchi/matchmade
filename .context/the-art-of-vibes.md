Good instincts. Both points are *true*, *unsolvable in full*, and actually strengthen the product if handled honestly.

Below is how to incorporate them **without overexplaining**, **without killing momentum**, and **without pretending we can solve chemistry**.

---

## 1. “We can’t match vibes” — how to include this honestly

### North Star framing (internal)

Add this as a **constraint**, not a failure:

> This system does not promise chemistry.
> It increases the odds that chemistry has room to emerge.

Key internal rule:

* The product optimizes for **conditions**, not outcomes.
* Alignment ≠ spark.
* Vibe is discovered **only in person**.

That protects you legally, ethically, and philosophically.

---

### How the product handles vibe (quietly)

You already have the right tools:

* Enneagram (optional, suggestive, not definitive)
* Communication style
* Energy / tone inference
* Group-based, low-pressure meetings

Together these:

* Reduce *anti-vibe* mismatches
* Do not claim to guarantee vibe

That’s the correct bar.

---

### Landing page implication (not explanation)

One short line, doing heavy work:

> Not every good match becomes a spark.
> But sparks need the right conditions to exist.

This does three things:

* Acknowledges reality
* Sets expectations
* Builds trust by *not overpromising*

---

## 2. Attraction grows with connection — how to incorporate without fighting attention span

This is subtle and powerful. It also differentiates you from swipe culture.

### North Star framing (internal)

Add this principle:

> Initial attraction is a filter, not a verdict.
> Connection is allowed to change the signal.

This justifies:

* Values-first ordering
* Private, binary attraction checks
* Slower reveal
* Meeting sooner

---

### Product behavior (already aligned)

You’re already doing this by:

* Separating values from photos
* Avoiding feeds and comparison
* Encouraging early real-world contact
* Avoiding “ranked attractiveness” systems

So you **don’t need to add features**.
You just need to **frame it correctly**.

---

### Landing page: how to allude without slowing readers down

Use **short, skimmable contrast lines**. These survive shrinking attention spans.

Example cluster (3 lines max):

> Attraction matters.
> But it isn’t static.
> And it isn’t everything.

Or:

> Sometimes attraction is instant.
> Sometimes it grows.
> We don’t rush either.

These are easy to scan and emotionally resonant.

---

## 3. Highlighting without headers (anti-skip tactics)

To prevent skipping:

### Use:

* Short standalone lines
* White space
* Sentence fragments
* Gentle repetition

### Avoid:

* Paragraph density
* Explanatory blocks
* Feature lists

Example pattern:

> We don’t promise chemistry.
> We promise honesty.

That line alone carries:

* Vibe realism
* Ethical positioning
* Brand confidence

---

## 4. Where this fits in the North Star brief (final additions)

Add two explicit truths:

**Truth #1 — Chemistry is discovered, not predicted**

> The system reduces bad matches but does not simulate human chemistry. Meeting in person remains essential.

**Truth #2 — Attraction is allowed to evolve**

> The product is designed to avoid premature rejection by prioritizing alignment before appearance, without removing attraction from the process.

These truths:

* Lower user defensiveness
* Increase credibility
* Prevent disappointment-driven churn

---

## 5. Do we *need* to include this?

Yes — but **lightly**.

Why:

* Users already know this subconsciously
* Saying it earns trust
* Overexplaining would kill momentum

Your instinct is right:
**implication > explication**
